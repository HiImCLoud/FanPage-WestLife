Westlife

Westlife is an iconic Irish pop group formed in Dublin in 1998. Known for their timeless music, they have sold over 55 million records and are one of the world's best-selling music artists. The group has received multiple accolades and has a massive fan base worldwide.

After a brief disbandment in 2012, Westlife reunited in 2018 and continues to captivate fans with their powerful ballads and pop hits. The band consists of Shane Filan, Mark Feehily, Kian Egan, and Nicky Byrne, with former member Brian McFadden leaving the group in 2004.

Westlife, initially formed as a five-member boy band, became known for their heart-touching ballads and hit singles. The group took the music world by storm in the late '90s, and their blend of harmony and smooth pop was admired globally. Albums like *Westlife* (1999), *Coast to Coast* (2000), and *World of Our Own* (2001) solidified their place in pop history.

The group has released twelve studio albums: four as a five-piece and eight as a four-piece. They rose to fame with their debut international self-titled studio album, Westlife (1999). It was followed by Coast to Coast (2000), World of Our Own (2001), and Turnaround (2003).

Following the departure of McFadden, the group released the cover albums ...Allow Us to Be Frank (2004) and The Love Album (2006), the albums Face to Face (2005), Back Home (2007), Where We Are (2009), and Gravity (2010), followed by an eight-year split. After reforming in 2018, the quartet released the studio albums Spectrum (2019) and Wild Dreams (2021).
